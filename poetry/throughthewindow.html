Through the Window


I

I have only this picture.

Pinched and brittle in your blue sailor dress
legs like broomsticks, how did you even stand?
the figure beside you cut out
except a red polyester arm around your shoulders,
all that is left to you of the aunt who sinned.
You looked up as if you looked for angels and puffy clouds
but through the window saw departing airplanes
the statue of the Virgin Mary in the yard.

II

A moth drawn to its flame
through the window I watch you:
a fly against a window
the bird who thinks it can fly through
the little girl who cried
because Jesus died for her and she wasn’t worth it.

III

Tears wash nothing.
Salty and thick,
their shame is blood
congealed and darkened
twisted into an unhealed scar.
Pain a whisper I cannot hear,
I reach to touch you
but the window stops my hand.
